FI-WARE

The platform for the Future Internet

FI-WARE is the Open Source platform designed to build Future Internet applications. It offer different type of services (Generic Enabler) that can be composed to create applications. FI-WARE includes Big Data, Internet of Things, Media services and much more. CREATE-NET, ENGINEERING, ORANGE, THALES and UPM contributes to FI-WARE.


Learn more on FI-WARE

FI-Lab

The FI-WARE instance open to everyone

FI-Lab is an instance of FI-WARE accessible for free and based on a Cloud infrastructure distributed around Europe. FI-Labs is concived to allow start-ups, SMEs and Web developers to test FI-WARE technology. CREATE-NET and FOKUS contributes to FI-Lab capacities.

What is the FI-PPP?

The FI-PPP programme is an innovation initiative funded by the European Commission and lead by the most important ICT industries. The iniative aims a developing and deploying a technology platform to support the development of Future Internet applications: FI-WARE. FI-WARE provides a set of cloud-based services (called Generic Enabler reference implementations) that can be used as building blocks to create complex applications. The second phase of FI-PPP started in 2013, in this phase the project XIFI provides the cloud infrastructures (FI-Lab) to host FI-WARE services, while other 5 projects develop specific domain platforms (i.e. manufacturing, e-health, content and media, energy and logistic) reusing the services provided by FI-WARE platform. In septemer 2014, the open calls for the third phase of the FI-PPP will start. In this phase 16 accelerator projects will provide 80 million euro for competitive open calls to SMEs and start-ups, with the aim to develop commercial applications based on FI-WARE and FI-PPP technologies. The FI-PPP Liasion activity promoted by EIT-ICT Labs, is an iniative to foster singergies between FI-PPP and EIT-ICT Labs.
